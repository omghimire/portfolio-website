Global Temperature rise and and extreme weather events

Global temperatures have risen by approximately 1.1°C since the pre-industrial era, primarily due to greenhouse gas emissions from human activities. This warming intensifies extreme weather events, including heatwaves, hurricanes, droughts, and heavy rainfall.

Heatwaves are becoming more frequent and severe, with record-breaking temperatures occurring worldwide. In 2023, many regions, including Europe and North America, experienced deadly heatwaves, stressing health systems and agriculture.

Hurricanes and Cyclones grow stronger as warmer ocean waters provide more energy. Storms like Hurricane Ian (2022) and Cyclone Freddy (2023) caused unprecedented destruction due to higher wind speeds and rainfall.

Droughts and Wildfires worsen with rising temperatures, drying soils and vegetation. Prolonged droughts in Africa and the western U.S. threaten food security, while wildfires in Canada and Australia burn larger areas than ever before.

Heavy Rainfall and Flooding increase because a warmer atmosphere holds more moisture. Events like Pakistan’s catastrophic 2022 floods, which displaced millions, are becoming more common.

Without urgent climate action, these extremes will escalate, endangering lives, economies, and ecosystems. Mitigation through emission reductions and adaptation strategies is critical to limit future damage.
